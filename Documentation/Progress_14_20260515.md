## Summary

Plan 14 swaps the retrieval model's query-side modification input from typed
text to spoken audio. FACap modifications are text-only (~55k strings), so they
must be synthesized to speech offline, once. Plan 14 §3.2/§3.3 deliberately
left the **TTS engine** undecided — "settled in the pilot, picked by listening
QC." This is that pilot.

We synthesized **5 representative FACap dress-slice captions** with **3
candidate voice-cloning TTS models** (F5-TTS, Chatterbox, Higgs Audio V2), all
zero-shot-cloned from **one shared reference voice**, WER-scored every clip, and
packaged the 15 clips into a self-contained interactive HTML listening demo.

**Outcome:**

- All three candidates are **tied on intelligibility** — avg WER ≈ 0.9%, and
  every caption scores 0.0% for every model except the longest (4.3%, a
  transcription-normalization artifact, not a real error). The words survive
  synthesis intact in all three.
- Because WER does not separate them, the engine was chosen on **voice
  naturalness and prosody** by listening QC.
- **Decision: Chatterbox** (Resemble AI, ~0.5B) is the synthesis engine for
  Plan 14 M2's full ~55k-utterance synthesis.

## 1. Context — why a TTS pilot

Plan 14's audio query tower ingests speech directly through speechQwen2VL's
Whisper encoder — there is no ASR step. The training input is therefore audio,
but FACap ships only text modifications. The plan's data design (§3) is to
manufacture the audio offline with a voice-cloning TTS driven by a multi-speaker
reference bank.

Two things had to be settled before the full ~55k synthesis:

1. **Which TTS engine** produces speech that is both intelligible (the
   modification's words must survive — they carry the retrieval signal) and
   natural (a smaller synthetic→real gap for later generalization).
2. Whether any local model is even runnable under this server's CUDA
   constraints.

This pilot answers both. It is the engine-selection sub-step of Plan 14
milestone M2; the VCTK reference bank and the full synthesis remain ahead.

## 2. Pilot design

### 2.1 The 5 captions

Sampled from the **FACap dataset, dress slice** — real natural-language
modification strings ("change dress A into dress B"), the same text that is the
query-side input to the two-tower model. The slice has ~55k of them, median
~90 characters. The 5 were chosen to span the range the full synthesis will hit:

| Tag | Len | Caption | Why this one |
|---|---|---|---|
| `shortest` | 93 | "The dress is smoother and lacks any pattern, while maintaining the same silhouette and color." | Shortest in the dress slice — the floor of the length range. |
| `longest` | 268 | "The dress is sleeveless and has a flared silhouette, featuring a mix of solid and patterned fabrics, with a distinct transition from a floral navy blue to a solid white and a paisley design, contrasting with the first dress's off-the-shoulder style and puffed sleeves." | A multi-clause stress test for length and coherence. |
| `fashion-vocab-1` | 172 | "The dress is longer and features a high neckline, with a pleated bodice and short sleeves adorned with ruffled lace trim, while the fabric appears lighter and more flowing." | Fashion-vocabulary dense — "pleated bodice", "ruffled lace trim". |
| `fashion-vocab-2` | 154 | "The dress is sleeveless and knee-length, featuring a V-neckline and a fitted silhouette, with a ruffle detail made of sheer fabric and a lace-trimmed hem." | Fashion-vocabulary dense — "V-neckline", "sheer fabric", "lace-trimmed hem". |
| `fashion-vocab-3` | 173 | "The dress is sleeveless and features a sweetheart neckline, with a smooth satin-like fabric instead of textured lace, and has a flared skirt rather than a fitted silhouette." | Fashion-vocabulary dense — "sweetheart neckline", "satin-like fabric". |

### 2.2 The shared reference voice

Every candidate here is a **zero-shot voice-cloning TTS**: given a short
reference clip, it clones that speaker's timbre and reads new text in that
voice. For the pilot, **one shared reference clip** was used for all 3 models
and all 5 captions, so any audible difference is attributable to the *model*,
not the voice choice.

Reference clip: F5-TTS's bundled English reference (`basic_ref_en.wav`,
24 kHz mono, ~6 s), content "Some call me nature, others call me mother
nature."

This single voice is a **pilot device only**. The Plan-14 training set will
*not* use one voice — it draws ~110 distinct speakers from VCTK (gender-
balanced, multiple English accents), per Plan 14 §3.1/§3.2, so the audio query
tower learns content speaker-invariantly.

### 2.3 Candidate models

The shortlist was informed by a web search for 2026 English TTS SOTA, filtered
to quality-first, locally-runnable, zero-shot voice cloning:

| Model | Params | Origin | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| F5-TTS | ~0.33B | SWivid | ran |
| Chatterbox | ~0.5B | Resemble AI | ran — **selected** |
| Higgs Audio V2 | ~5.8B | Boson AI | ran (after a version-drift fix, §3.4) |
| IndexTTS-2 | ~1–2B | (index-tts) | blocked — CUDA driver (§3.5) |

Higgs was included specifically as the "does a much bigger model surprise us?"
check against the two small models.

## 3. Synthesis — environment, and what ran

### 3.1 Storage discipline

The entire pilot — conda envs, HF checkpoint caches, pip/conda package caches,
synthesized audio — was placed on the local SSD `/tmp3/zhuoyuan/tts_pilot/`.
`HF_HOME`, `PIP_CACHE_DIR`, `CONDA_PKGS_DIRS`, `XDG_CACHE_HOME`, `TORCH_HOME`
were all redirected there via a sourced `env.sh`. Verified afterward: nothing
landed in the NAS home directory — `~/.cache` was untouched; the only home
footprint is the deliverable `~/tts_pilot_demo/` (~9 MB of audio + the HTML).

### 3.2 The CUDA constraint that shaped the shortlist

The server's GPU driver is CUDA 12.4. This caps usable torch wheels at the
`cu124` build (torch ≤ 2.6); newer `cu128`/`cu130` wheels fail at runtime. This
constraint decided which models ran and which were blocked.

### 3.3 Models that ran — per-model fixes

- **Chatterbox** — its `perth` audio-watermarker imports `pkg_resources`, which
  `setuptools` 82 removed. Fixed by pinning `setuptools<81` in its env.
- **F5-TTS** — default `pip install` pulled `torch 2.12+cu130` (driver too
  old). Fixed by pinning `torch==2.6.0` / `torchaudio==2.6.0` from the `cu124`
  index and removing `torchcodec`.
- **Higgs Audio V2** — see §3.4.

Each model got its own isolated conda env under `/tmp3/.../envs/`.

### 3.4 The Higgs Audio V2 version-drift fix

Higgs was the hardest to run. The standalone `boson_multimodal` serving code
(the `higgs-audio` repo) could not load the published checkpoint. Diagnosis:
**both** Higgs HF repos were migrated, in 2026, to a native-`transformers`
format the standalone code predates —

- The checkpoint `bosonai/higgs-audio-v2-generation-3B-base` `main` HEAD now
  ships a flat config with `model_type: higgs_audio_v2`, `architectures:
  ['HiggsAudioV2ForConditionalGeneration']`, `transformers_version: 5.3.0.dev0`.
  The `boson_multimodal` code expects the old nested config with `model_type:
  higgs_audio` and an `audio_encoder_config` block.
- The audio tokenizer `bosonai/higgs-audio-v2-tokenizer` was likewise migrated
  (a `2026-04-04 trfms-support` commit), adding an `acoustic_model_config` key
  the repo's `HiggsAudioTokenizer.__init__` does not accept.

Fix: pin each repo to its **last pre-migration revision** and download to a
local directory the serving code reads directly —

- checkpoint → revision `7cfe9946a0` (2025-07-22; old nested `higgs_audio`
  config)
- tokenizer → revision `9d4988fbd4` (2025-07-22; old flat-key config)

With both pinned, `HiggsAudioServeEngine` loaded and synthesized all 5 captions
cleanly.

### 3.5 IndexTTS-2 — driver-blocked, not run

IndexTTS-2's dependencies pin `torch==2.8.*` from the `cu128` index, which
needs a CUDA 12.8 driver. This server is 12.4 (§3.2). Running it would require
a newer-driver host; deferred.

## 4. Results

### 4.1 WER — intelligibility (faster-whisper `medium.en` + `jiwer`)

Each clip was transcribed with `faster-whisper medium.en` and WER computed
against the normalized source caption (lower-cased, punctuation stripped).
Low WER = the modification's words survived synthesis = the retrieval signal is
intact.

| Model | avg WER | per-caption |
|---|---|---|
| F5-TTS | ~0.9% | shortest 0.0 · longest 4.3 · fv-1/2/3 0.0 |
| Chatterbox | ~0.9% | shortest 0.0 · longest 4.3 · fv-1/2/3 0.0 |
| Higgs Audio V2 | ~0.9% | shortest 0.0 · longest 4.3 · fv-1/2/3 0.0 |

The three models are **identical** caption-by-caption: every caption is 0.0%
except `longest` at 4.3%, and that 4.3% is the same jiwer/normalization artifact
for all three (a punctuation/word-boundary difference, not a dropped word).
**Intelligibility is perfectly tied — and not the deciding factor.** The bigger
model (Higgs, ~5.8B) does not win on word-accuracy over the ~0.33–0.5B models.

### 4.2 Listening QC

The 15 clips (3 models × 5 captions) were packaged into a self-contained
interactive HTML demo (§6) and listened to end to end. Since WER is tied, the
audible differences — voice naturalness, prosody, pacing, and clean handling of
fashion vocabulary — were the basis for selection.

## 5. Decision — Chatterbox

**Chatterbox (Resemble AI, ~0.5B) is selected** as the synthesis engine for
Plan 14 M2.

The decision was made by listening QC on the demo, since the WER scores left
the three candidates indistinguishable on intelligibility. Chatterbox was the
preferred voice on naturalness/prosody for these fashion-modification captions.

## 7. How Chatterbox controls the voice — relevant to M2

Verified from `ChatterboxTTS.generate`:

```
generate(text, repetition_penalty=1.2, min_p=0.05, top_p=1.0,
         audio_prompt_path=None, exaggeration=0.5, cfg_weight=0.5,
         temperature=0.8)
```

| Parameter | Controls | Note |
|---|---|---|
| `audio_prompt_path` | **WHO** — timbre, speaker, gender, accent | This *is* the voice. There is no timbre knob — swap the reference clip to swap the voice. |
| `exaggeration` (0.5) | expressiveness / emotion intensity | how dramatic the delivery is |
| `cfg_weight` (0.5) | pacing / guidance strength | lower → slower, more deliberate |
| `temperature` (0.8) | prosodic randomness | with `repetition_penalty`, `min_p`, `top_p` |

Implication for the full synthesis (Plan 14 M2, `src/data/build_tts_audio.py`):

- Voice diversity comes entirely from the **reference clip**, so the ~110-voice
  spec is met with the **VCTK reference bank** — one short clean clip per
  speaker, ~10 speakers held out for the separate OOD-voice eval.
- Each of the ~55k FACap modifications is assigned a VCTK speaker; Chatterbox
  clones that speaker's clip and reads the modification.
- `exaggeration` / `temperature` / `cfg_weight` can be lightly jittered per
  utterance for natural variety (Plan 14 §3.1's "light per-utterance jitter").
- Chatterbox needs only the reference *clip*, not its transcript (unlike
  F5-TTS), so the VCTK bank is just audio files — simpler to build.

## 11. M1 — image+audio multimodal-forward smoke test (2026-05-15)

Plan 14 §6's gate before any tower work. speechQwen2VL had only ever been fed
*audio + text* (ASR); Qwen2-VL natively does *image + text*; **`image + audio`
in one message was untested.** `scripts/smoke_image_audio.py` loads
`DanJZY/Qwen2-VL-7B-Speech` and runs 13 checks in three groups — **all pass**:

- **Weights** — `audio_encoder` (637M-param Whisper encoder) + `audio_projector`
  (17.4M) present (at `model.audio_encoder` / `model.audio_projector`, nested
  under the inner `Qwen2VLModel`); zero audio weights among the missing keys;
  weights finite and non-zero; `config.audio_token_id = 151658`.
- **Single forward** — a `[image, audio, text]` user message tokenizes with
  334 audio tokens placed, `audio_features` `(1,128,3000)` consumed, finite
  last hidden state `(1,460,3584)`, finite pooled query embedding.
- **bs=2 padded** — `shortest.wav` → 334 audio tokens, `longest.wav` → 894
  (audio duration honored per-sample); batch padded to `(2,1180)`; finite
  hidden states; the two rows are distinct (cos 0.9466).

The audio enters via `process_vision_info` → `processor(..., audios=)` →
WhisperFeatureExtractor mel features → `audio_encoder` → `audio_projector` →
`masked_scatter` into the LM input embeds at audio-token positions. The
processor/model self-guard: a token/feature-count mismatch raises rather than
silently dropping audio. Test image from the FACap dress slice; test audio from
the Chatterbox pilot outputs (§4).

### How each check is decided

- **Weights loaded.** The base is loaded with
  `from_pretrained(..., output_loading_info=True)`, which returns the model
  *and* a `loading_info` dict. `loading_info["missing_keys"]` lists parameters
  the architecture declared but the checkpoint did not supply (those silently
  keep their random init). The check filters `missing_keys` for
  `audio_encoder` / `audio_projector` names and passes iff that list is empty —
  i.e. every audio parameter actually came from the checkpoint. The two modules
  are then located by walking `model.named_modules()` (robust to the nesting
  under the inner `Qwen2VLModel`); all their weights are flattened and checked
  `torch.isfinite(...).all()` (no NaN/Inf) and `.abs().sum() > 0` (not
  degenerate-zero). `missing_keys`-empty is what proves "loaded, not init"; the
  finite / non-zero checks are a sanity floor; the *functional* proof is the
  forward itself.
- **Forward outputs.** Each assertion is `torch.isfinite(tensor).all()` over
  the whole tensor (last hidden state, pooled embedding). Audio-token presence
  is `(input_ids == audio_token_id).sum() > 0`.
- **bs=2 padding.** Two samples with different audio lengths are run through
  `processor(..., padding=True)`. The batch width `1180` is *emergent*, not
  targeted — it is the longer sample's total length (its image tokens + 894
  audio tokens + instruction/template tokens); the shorter sample (460 tokens
  on its own) is padded up to it, with the `attention_mask` marking the pad
  positions. The check asserts the batch dimension is 2 and the batched hidden
  state is fully finite — exercising the variable-length-audio padding path
  that a single-sample forward cannot reach.

A shared `check(name, ok, detail)` helper prints `[PASS]`/`[FAIL]` and records
failures; the script exits non-zero if any check fails (all 13 passed, exit 0).

**Plan 14 §6 gate cleared — tower work may proceed.**

## 12. M2 — VCTK reference bank + Chatterbox synthesis (2026-05-15 / 16)

Plan 14 §3/§5's data step: turn the text-only FACap dress modifications into
speech. `src/data/build_tts_audio.py` (new) drives it in three modes —
`bank` / `synth` / `manifest`.

**Reference bank.** VCTK-Corpus-0.92 (~11.7 GB) downloaded and extracted to
`/tmp3/zhuoyuan/plan14_audio/vctk`. `bank` mode picks one clean reference clip
per speaker (the clip whose duration is closest to 7 s, resampled to 24 kHz
mono) and splits the 110 speakers into a **100-speaker training pool + 10
held-out OOD speakers**, gender-balanced (5 M / 5 F in the OOD set). Chatterbox
is a zero-shot voice clone, so the clip *is* the voice (§7). Writes
`ref_bank/<spk>.wav` + `bank.json`.

**Synthesis plan.** FACap dress-train has 59,082 triplets; mirroring
`FacapContrastiveDataset`'s deterministic split (seed 42) gives **55,186 used
triplets** (53,686 train + 500 dev + 1,000 headline). `bank` mode assigns each
a training-pool speaker and lightly-jittered Chatterbox knobs (exaggeration
0.40–0.55, cfg_weight 0.45–0.60, temperature 0.70–0.90 — per-utterance rate /
prosody variation, Plan 14 §3.1), and additionally assigns each dev+headline
triplet a held-out OOD speaker for the separate OOD-voice eval. Total
**56,686 jobs** (55,186 in-distribution + 1,500 OOD) → `synth_plan.json`.

**Synthesis.** `scripts/run_tts_synth.sh` launches `synth` mode as 8 shards,
one per vllab6 RTX 3090. Each shard streams its jobs through Chatterbox,
resamples 24 kHz → 16 kHz mono, and writes `audio/<idx>.wav` (in-distribution) /
`audio_ood/<idx>.wav`. Resumable — existing wavs are skipped, so an interrupted
run just continues. A 12-job sanity shard passed first (0 failures, valid
16 kHz mono wavs).

**Completed (2026-05-16).** All 8 shards finished cleanly in ~12.8 h
wall-clock — **56,686 / 56,686 clips synthesized, 0 failures** (55,186
in-distribution + 1,500 OOD-voice). `build_tts_audio.py manifest` collated
`manifest.json` (55,186 in-dist + 1,500 OOD records, triplet index + condition
→ wav / speaker / split; 0 planned wavs missing); a 6-clip random spot-check
confirmed valid 16 kHz mono wavs. Audio footprint ~14.4 GB at
`/tmp3/zhuoyuan/plan14_audio/{audio,audio_ood}`.
